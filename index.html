1. Overview

1.1 Problem Statement

COSOMIS currently provides detailed profile pages only at the Village level. Stakeholders responsible for Cantons (arrondissements) and Communes have no dedicated view to monitor planning progress, investment priorities, or geographic distribution of subprojects across the communities they oversee. Additionally, the platform lacks a centralized way to browse, filter, and download field-collected photos and documents attached to planning tasks.

1.2 Proposed Solution

Three new interfaces that extend the existing COSOMIS platform:

PrototypeLevelCore Purpose
Canton ProfileDirect parent of VillageAggregate village-level data and expose a planning progress funnel showing how each village is moving through the 4 planning phases
Commune ProfileParent of Canton (grandparent of Village)Two-tier aggregation — group villages by Canton — with filters, investment tracking, and an interactive map with switchable layers
GalleryCross-cutting (all levels)Searchable, filterable media browser for photos and documents attached to planning tasks, with lightbox viewing and bulk download

1.3 Target Users

UserRolePrimary Need
Canton Chief / Arrondissement OfficerLocal government officialTrack which villages have completed their planning cycle and identify lagging ones
Commune Mayor / Municipal OfficerMunicipal authorityOverview of all cantons, funding gaps, geographic priority distribution
World Bank Project ManagerDonor / oversightPortfolio-wide progress dashboards, evidence of field activities via gallery
Facilitator / Field AgentOperational staffUpload and browse evidence, verify task completion through photos

2. Administrative Hierarchy

The COSOMIS data model uses a self-referencing AdministrativeLevel entity with a parent foreign key. The hierarchy, from top to bottom:

Key design principle: The planning cycle (Phases → Activities → Tasks) is tracked at the Village level only. At Canton and Commune levels, the system shows a progress funnel — an aggregation of how many child villages have completed, are in progress, or have not started each phase.

3. Canton Profile

Open prototype →

3.1 Purpose

The Canton Profile gives canton-level authorities a single view to understand the state of all villages under their jurisdiction. It answers: "How are my villages doing overall, where are the investment priorities, and which villages need attention?"

3.2 Layout

SectionDescription
BreadcrumbBenin / Department / Commune / Canton Name — with links to parent levels
Image CarouselPhotos from the canton's villages (from Attachment model). Overlay shows canton name and parent hierarchy.
Population CardDemographic breakdown: total, men, women, young, elder, persons with disabilities, agriculture, breeders, ethnic groups
Villages Summary CardQuick stats: number of villages, CVDs, priorities identified, total estimated cost

3.3 Tabs

Tab: Priorities

Shows all investment priorities from villages that belong to this canton. Aggregates Investment records where administrative_level.parent = canton. Columns include ranking, sector, description, endorsement indicators (Youth, Women, Ethnic Minorities, Agriculturists), beneficiaries, estimated cost, funding source, climate contribution, and cart actions for unfunded priorities.

Tab: Villages

Lists all villages grouped by their CVD (Comité Villageois de Développement). Shows population, planning status, number of priorities, and languages spoken. Includes a text search to filter the table.

Tab: Village Progress

The core innovation replacing the village-level "Planning Cycle" tab. Since planning phases only exist at village level, this tab provides a funnel view showing how each village is progressing through the 4 planning phases.

Why a funnel? Canton officials don't need to manage individual tasks — they need to know which villages are stuck and where resources should be redirected. The funnel immediately highlights bottlenecks.

Components:

ComponentDescription
Summary counters4 stat cards: villages completed, in progress, not started, and overall completion %
Per-village progress barsHorizontal stacked bars showing completed (green), in progress (yellow), and not started (red) phases for each village
Phase breakdown tableMatrix of villages × 4 phases with status dots, plus priorities identified count

Tab: Map

Interactive Leaflet.js map showing all villages within the canton. Each village is represented by a circle marker colored by its top-priority sector. Clicking a marker opens a popup with the priority name, estimated cost, and funding status. A sidebar lists all subprojects, and clicking an item flies the map to that village.

Tab: Infrastructure & Services

Placeholder for future development. Will show infrastructure inventory across villages (schools, health centers, water points, roads).

3.4 Metric Calculations

Total Population
SUM(village.total_population) for village in canton.children
Aggregated from all child villages' demographic data
Overall Completion %
SUM(village_completed_phases) / (total_villages × 4) × 100
Each village has 4 phases. Count all completed phases across all villages, divide by theoretical max.
Villages Completed
COUNT(village) WHERE all 4 phases.status == 'completed'
A village is "completed" only when all 4 phases have status = completed
Villages In Progress
COUNT(village) WHERE any phase.status == 'in progress' AND NOT all_completed
At least one phase started but not all 4 are completed
Villages Not Started
COUNT(village) WHERE all phases.status == 'not started'
No phase has been started in this village
Total Estimated Cost
SUM(investment.estimated_cost) for investment in canton_investments
Sum of all Investment records linked to villages under this canton
Phase Status (per village)
phase.get_status() → checks tasks within activities
A phase is completed when all its activities' tasks are completed. In progress when at least one task is completed but not all. Not started when no tasks are completed. Derived from Task.status field.
Priorities Identified
COUNT(Investment) WHERE investment.administrative_level.parent == canton
Number of investment records linked to child villages

4. Commune Profile

Open prototype →

4.1 Purpose

The Commune Profile provides municipal-level oversight across all cantons. It answers: "Which cantons are on track, where is investment needed most, and how do I compare subproject distribution geographically?"

4.2 Layout

SectionDescription
Image CarouselPhotos from the commune's territory, with overlay showing commune name and department
Population CardSame demographic breakdown as Canton, aggregated across all villages in the commune
Commune Overview CardNumber of cantons, villages, priorities identified, total estimated cost, communal development plan download link, and assigned facilitator
Summary Stats Row4 highlight cards: cantons count, villages count, priorities identified, total estimated cost

4.3 Tabs

Tab: Priorities

Two sub-views toggled by pills:

Sub-viewDescription
Investment TypesAggregated by investment type and sector across the entire commune. Shows funding need, endorsement columns (J/F/ME/AE), and funding status.
Individual VillagesFlat list of every village-level priority with its canton, sector, endorsements, village ranking, and estimated cost.

Both sub-views share a filter bar with 5 dropdowns: Sector, Type, Endorsed By, Climate Contribution, and Funding Status. Apply / Reset actions.

Tab: Villages

Full table of all villages in the commune with columns: name, parent canton (linked to canton profile), population, invested capital, required capital, per capita invested, and per capita required. Per-capita metrics allow comparing investment equity across villages.

Tab: Cantons

Aggregated view at the canton level showing: canton name (linked to canton profile), number of villages, population, priorities count, invested amount, required amount, and planning status with completion percentage.

Tab: Canton Progress

Two-tier progress funnel. At the top, commune-wide summary counters (villages completed, in progress, not started, overall %). Below, each canton is an expandable accordion block with a header showing the canton name, village count, completion %, mini progress bar, and status counts. Expanding a canton reveals per-village progress bars — identical to the Canton Profile's funnel, but nested inside the commune view.

Two-tier aggregation: Commune → Canton → Village. This is the key difference from the Canton Profile. The collapsible structure prevents information overload while still allowing drill-down to any village.

Tab: Map

Interactive Leaflet.js map with three switchable layers:

LayerMarker ColorPurpose
PrioritiesBy sector (Education = orange, Health = green, Water = cyan, Agriculture = blue, Transport = gray)Understand spatial distribution of priority types
SubprojectsSame sector colors, larger semi-transparent ringsHighlight only villages with active subprojects
Planning ProgressGreen (completed), Yellow (in progress), Red (not started)Identify geographic clusters of stalled planning

The sidebar groups subprojects by canton, and clicking an item flies the map to that location and opens the popup.

Tab: Subprojects

Table of all subprojects in the commune with ranking, sector, endorsement indicators, beneficiary count, estimated cost, responsible structure, and climate contribution.

4.4 Metric Calculations

Population (all fields)
SUM(village.field) for village in commune.descendants(type='village')
Recursively sums across all villages in all cantons under this commune
Overall Completion %
SUM(all_village_completed_phases) / (total_villages_in_commune × 4) × 100
Same formula as Canton but across all villages in all cantons
Per Capita Invested
village.total_funded / village.total_population
How much investment each person in that village has received. Used to identify equity gaps.
Per Capita Required
village.total_estimated_cost / village.total_population
Investment still needed per person. High values indicate underserved villages.
Canton Completion %
SUM(completed_phases in canton) / (canton_village_count × 4) × 100
Planning completion rate scoped to a single canton
Total Estimated Cost
SUM(investment.estimated_cost) for all investments in commune
Sum across all Investment records where administrative_level is descendant of commune

6. User Stories

6.1 Canton Profile Stories

US-C01 — Canton overview
"As a Canton Chief, I want to see a summary of all villages under my jurisdiction so that I can quickly assess the overall population and number of priorities."
Acceptance Criteria
  • Population card shows aggregated demographics (total, men, women, young, elder, disabilities, agriculture, breeders, ethnic groups) summed from all child villages
  • Villages summary card shows total village count, CVD count, priority count, and total estimated cost
  • Image carousel displays photos from at least one child village's attachments, or a default image
US-C02 — Village progress funnel
"As a Canton Chief, I want to see which villages have completed their planning cycle and which are lagging, so that I can focus my support on villages that need it most."
Acceptance Criteria
  • Summary counters show correct counts for completed, in-progress, and not-started villages
  • Overall completion % is calculated as total completed phases / (village_count × 4) × 100
  • Each village shows a horizontal stacked bar proportional to phases completed / in progress / not started
  • Phase breakdown table shows each of the 4 phases per village with a colored status dot
  • Village names are clickable links to the village profile page
US-C03 — Canton investment map
"As a Canton Chief, I want to see where investment priorities are located on a map so that I can understand the geographic distribution of needs."
Acceptance Criteria
  • Map renders with OpenStreetMap tiles centered on the canton's geographic bounds
  • Each village with a priority shows a circle marker colored by sector
  • Clicking a marker shows popup with: village name, sector, priority description, estimated cost, funding status
  • Sidebar lists all subprojects; clicking an item pans/zooms the map to that village
  • Legend shows sector-to-color mapping
US-C04 — Canton priorities table
"As a World Bank PM, I want to review all investment priorities in a canton with their endorsement status, so that I can evaluate whether vulnerable groups are represented."
Acceptance Criteria
  • Priorities table shows all investments from child villages with endorsement indicators (J, F, ME, AE)
  • Funded priorities display their funding source; unfunded show "Add to cart" action
  • Table supports pagination (DataTables, 10 rows per page)

6.2 Commune Profile Stories

US-M01 — Canton-aggregated progress
"As a Commune Mayor, I want to see planning progress grouped by canton so that I can compare cantons and know which ones are falling behind."
Acceptance Criteria
  • Each canton appears as a collapsible accordion block with header showing: canton name, village count, completion %, mini progress bar, and status counts
  • Expanding a canton reveals per-village progress bars identical to the Canton Profile funnel
  • Summary counters at the top aggregate all villages across all cantons
  • At least one canton is expanded by default to demonstrate the drill-down pattern
US-M02 — Investment equity analysis
"As a Commune Mayor, I want to compare per-capita investment across villages so that I can identify underserved communities."
Acceptance Criteria
  • Villages tab shows per capita invested (total_funded / population) and per capita required (estimated_cost / population)
  • Villages with zero invested capital are clearly visible (showing 0)
  • Each village row links to canton and village profiles
US-M03 — Multi-layer map
"As a World Bank PM, I want to switch between different map views (priorities, subprojects, planning progress) so that I can analyze geographic patterns from different angles."
Acceptance Criteria
  • Three toggle buttons switch the active map layer: Priorities, Subprojects, Planning Progress
  • Only one layer is visible at a time; active button is highlighted
  • Legend updates dynamically to match the active layer's color scheme
  • Sidebar groups items by canton with section headers
US-M04 — Priority filtering
"As a Municipal Officer, I want to filter investment priorities by sector, endorsement group, and funding status so that I can prepare targeted reports."
Acceptance Criteria
  • Filter bar provides 5 dropdowns: Sector, Type, Endorsed By, Climate Contribution, Funding Status
  • Apply button submits the filters; Reset clears all
  • Table updates to show only matching investments
  • Sub-tabs (Investment Types / Individual Villages) both respect the same filters

6.3 Gallery Stories

US-G01 — Browse field evidence
"As a World Bank PM, I want to browse all photos collected from the field filtered by location and planning phase, so that I can verify activities are actually taking place."
Acceptance Criteria
  • Gallery grid shows photo thumbnails with village name, task title, status, and date
  • Filter sidebar allows filtering by department → commune → village (cascading), and phase → activity → task
  • Active filters appear as removable chips above the grid
  • Gallery count text updates to reflect filtered results (e.g. "Showing 12 photos in Abomey-Calavi")
US-G02 — Full-screen preview
"As a Facilitator, I want to view photos in a full-screen lightbox with navigation, so that I can review task evidence one by one without leaving the page."
Acceptance Criteria
  • Clicking a card (not the checkbox) opens a full-screen lightbox
  • Lightbox shows current/total counter (e.g. "3 / 12")
  • Previous/Next buttons navigate between photos; Arrow keys and Escape also work
  • Image metadata (task name, village, date) is displayed below the image
  • Download button allows saving the individual photo
US-G03 — Bulk download
"As a World Bank PM, I want to select multiple photos and download them as a ZIP file, so that I can include field evidence in my reports."
Acceptance Criteria
  • Each card has a checkbox that becomes visible on hover or when checked
  • When at least one item is checked, a floating action bar appears at the bottom with: selection count, "Download ZIP" button, and "Deselect" button
  • Download ZIP packages all selected attachments
  • Deselect clears all checkboxes and hides the action bar
US-G04 — Empty state & recovery
"As any user, when my filters return no results, I want a clear message and a way to reset filters so that I'm not stuck on a blank screen."
Acceptance Criteria
  • When no results match, an empty state shows an icon, message "No photos found", helpful suggestion text, and a "Clear filters" button
  • Clicking "Clear filters" resets all filter selections and reloads the full gallery

8. Hypotheses

#HypothesisEvidence NeededRisk if Wrong
H1 Canton and Commune officials will use the progress funnel more than the raw village list to identify lagging villages, because the visual bar chart communicates status faster than reading a table. Tab click analytics: Village Progress / Canton Progress tab should have higher engagement time than Villages tab. Low. The funnel coexists with the table, so users can choose either.
H2 The interactive map will be the primary discovery tool for geographic patterns. Users will identify clusters of unfunded or stalled villages by switching map layers rather than reading tables. Track layer toggle frequency. Users who toggle at least 2 layers per session are discovering patterns. Medium. If the map is slow to load or coordinates are missing, users will fall back to tables.
H3 The gallery's hierarchical location filter will be the most-used filter axis (over phase/activity), because users think in terms of geography first ("show me photos from village X"). Track which filter dropdowns are changed most frequently. Low. All filters remain available regardless.
H4 Bulk download (ZIP) will significantly reduce the time PMs spend preparing progress reports, replacing the current workflow of downloading photos one by one from individual village profiles. Compare report preparation time before/after feature launch via user interviews. Low. Even without ZIP, the gallery filter + lightbox is an improvement over navigating village by village.
H5 Two-tier aggregation (Commune → Canton → Village) will not cause information overload. Users will understand the collapsible accordion pattern and use it to drill down rather than being confused. Usability testing: >80% of test users can find a specific village's progress within 20 seconds. High. If the accordion is confusing, the Commune progress tab becomes useless. Mitigation: default one canton open.
H6 Per-capita investment metrics will surface equity issues that raw totals hide. Officials will use per-capita columns to advocate for underserved villages in budget discussions. Track whether villages with low per-capita investment receive higher future allocations. Medium. Per-capita may be confusing for some users. Tooltip explanations will help.
